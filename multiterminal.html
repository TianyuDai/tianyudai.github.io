Multi-terminal Josephson junctions

Multi-terminal Josephson junctions (JJs) have the ability to simulate crystal structures by cleverly exploiting the dependence of the Andreev spectrum on the periodic superconducting phase. It has been proposed that these systems can undergo topological phase transitions, including to the Weyl semimetal state, by tuning the relative phase of the superconducting contacts to zero energy points. With more contacts comes more tunability, allowing the Weyl points to be transformed into lines, planes etc. Thus, the study of multi-terminal junctions could allow for valuable insight into higher dimension crystal physics, quantum circuit elements, and exotic states of matter. edge modes â€“ to complete the bound state between the superconducting leads.

In our lab, we have studied the vital building blocks of supercurrent flow in multi-terminal junctions. We find that supercurrents and dissipative currents can exist in the same region of graphene, causing electron heating which is efficiently cooled by electron-phonon coupling.


By varying bias on three contacts of a multi-terminal device, we are able to see branches of supercurrent flow between different Josephson junctions. (See related publications for more information.)

Recommendation System: Product similarities via network analysis

supervised by Prof. Debmalya Panigrahi
Oct. 2019 - Feb. 2020

  • Implemented a ``seller-centric'' framework: performing network analysis on a product graph derived from descriptions and tags provided by sellers
  • Established a ``buyer-centric'' framework: generating neural network based analysis of co-purchase and co-use data provided by customers
  • Compared two frameworks via extensive experiments on realistic data extracted from STEAM website
  • Discovered that rich signals of user-generated data overcome sparsity and unreliability of product descriptions and tags in e-commerce applications

Bayesian Analysis: Quantify fundamental properties of relativistic particle collisions

supervised by Prof. Steffen A. Bass
June 2020 - present

  • Managed high-throughput computation to run large-scale simulations of relativistic particle collisions
  • Applied Bayesian analysis to perform a first systematic extraction of particle transport properties
  • Used a proof of principle calculation to understand how adding measurements, or reducing uncertainties can improve constraints on the transport coefficients

Image Recognition: Towards a Better Understanding of Data Imbalance

supervised by Prof. Debmalya Panigrahi and Prof. Rong Ge
March 2021 - May 2021

  • Examined class imbalance and feature imbalance on image-based dataset CIFAR-10 and CelebA
  • Trained several neural network instances of ResNet-18 and VGG16 with varied imbalance levels
  • Proposed an explanation based on Neural Collapse to understand the degradation on the minority classes caused by data imbalance
